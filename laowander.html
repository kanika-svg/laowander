Si Phan Don · 4,000 Islands
Southern Laos · Hidden Paradise

The island where time forgets to move.

Don Det is unlike anywhere else in Southeast Asia. No cars. No rush. Just the Mekong lapping at the shore, hammocks strung between palm trees, and sunsets that turn the river to liquid gold. This is where the rare Irrawaddy dolphins swim at dawn, where a bicycle is all the transport you need, and where every meal costs less than a coffee back home.

Most travellers pass through. The smart ones stay for a week.

Everything you need
to know before you go.

Visas, currency, the best time to visit, how to get around — the LaoWander travel guide covers it all.

01
🛂
Visa on Arrival
Most nationalities get a 30-day VOA for $30–42 USD. Apply for an e-Visa at laoevisa.gov.la to skip the queue — takes 3 business days.
02
💵
Money & Costs
Laos is exceptional value. Budget $25–40/day. USD and Thai Baht accepted widely. Carry cash in smaller towns — ATMs disappear.
03
🌤
Best Time to Visit
Oct–Feb is peak season — cool, dry, perfect. Oct–Nov is the sweet spot: post-rains green, waterfalls full, no crowds yet.
04
🚂
Laos-China Railway
Vientiane to Luang Prabang in under 2 hours. The 2022 rail link changed everything — clean, fast, spectacular views.
05
🙏
Culture & Etiquette
Cover up at temples, never touch anyone's head. A warm "Sabai dee!" opens every door in every village.
06
🏥
Health & Safety
Get travel insurance with medical evacuation. Never leave marked trails in rural provinces — UXO remains active.
