1Day
Vientiane
Arrive & discover the world's most relaxed capital
Ease into Laos with a gentle afternoon in Vientiane. The city moves at its own pace — let it set yours.
Vientiane · Mekong promenade
Afternoon
Arrive in Vientiane
Check in and walk to the Mekong riverside. The Patuxai Victory Monument is a 10-minute walk from most central hotels — climb it for city views.
FreeEasy
Sunset
Mekong Promenade at dusk
Walk Fa Ngum Road along the river as the sun drops behind the Thai mountains. Street vendors set up at sunset — grab a Beer Lao and a grilled skewer.
FreeUnmissable
Dinner
Dinner at MakPhet or Lao Kitchen
Your first proper Lao meal. Try the laap, tam mak hoong (papaya salad) and sticky rice. MakPhet for a special first night; Lao Kitchen for the local experience.
$5–18Must eat
🏨
Settha Palace Hotel
From $145/night · Central Vientiane
Tuk-tuk from airport ~$5
2Day
Vientiane
Temples, coffee & the Talat Sao night market
Explore Vientiane's Buddhist heritage by day and its best street food scene by night.
7:00 AM
Pha That Luang at dawn
The golden stupa of Laos — most sacred monument in the country. Arrive early before tour groups and you'll often have it almost to yourself. Dress respectfully.
$3 entryIconic
9:30 AM
Bolaven coffee at Rue Namphu Café
The colonial-era café on the Namphu fountain square. Perfect croissants and the best Bolaven espresso in the capital. Sit on the terrace and people-watch for an hour.
$3–8
11:00 AM
Wat Si Saket & COPE Visitor Centre
Wat Si Saket houses 6,800 Buddha images — the oldest surviving temple in Vientiane. COPE tells the powerful story of UXO victims and Laos' recovery. Free entry.
$2 entryEducational
Evening
Talat Sao Night Market
Fill a plate at the night market food stalls for under $2. Nam khao (crispy rice salad), noodle soups, grilled meats — this is Vientiane at its most alive.
$2–5Must do
🏨
Settha Palace Hotel
2nd night · Central Vientiane
3Day
Vientiane → Luang Prabang
The train north — arriving in a different century
The Laos-China railway makes this one of the best travel days in Southeast Asia. Under 2 hours, spectacular scenery, $8.
Morning
Laos-China Railway to Luang Prabang
Book the morning train from Vientiane (Khambang station). The 1hr 50min journey through tunnels and jungle is an experience in itself. Sit on the left side for the best views.
$8–12Highlight
Afternoon
Old Town walk — Wat Xieng Thong
Check in and walk the UNESCO heritage old town. Wat Xieng Thong is Luang Prabang's most beautiful temple — the golden mosaic on the rear wall is breathtaking.
$3 entryUNESCO
Evening
Sisavangvong Night Market
Fill a plate at Luang Prabang's legendary nightly market for 15,000 LAK (~$0.70). Over 100 vendor stalls serving the best cheap Lao food in the country. Arrive by 6pm.
$0.70Legendary
🏨
Ock Pop Tok or Riverside Boutique
From $78–95/night
Train $8 · Book ahead
4Day
Luang Prabang
Alms ceremony, Phousi Hill & Tat Kuang Si waterfall
The most complete day you can have in Luang Prabang — from dawn ritual to turquoise waterfalls.
5:30 AM
Tak Bat — the alms giving ceremony
Stand respectfully on Sakkaline Road as saffron-robed monks collect alms at dawn. Watch, don't photograph with flash, and never touch the monks. One of Asia's most moving rituals.
FreeOnce in a lifetime
8:00 AM
Breakfast at Blue Lagoon Bakery
The best croissants outside Paris, made by a Lao family trained in French patisserie. Quiet courtyard, strong Bolaven coffee. Perfect after the early start.
$4–8
9:30 AM
Tat Kuang Si Waterfall
30km from town — take a tuk-tuk ($10 return). The turquoise tiered pools are extraordinary. Swim in the lower pools, hike to the top falls. Arrive before 10am to beat the crowds.
$3 entry + $10 tuk-tukMust see
Sunset ⛰️
Phousi Hill sunset
328 steps to the summit for a 360° view over the old town, the Mekong and the mountains. The most photographed sunset in Laos. Get there 30 minutes early for a good spot.
$3 entryIconic view
🏨
Same hotel · 2nd night LP
Luang Prabang
5Day
Luang Prabang
Pak Ou Caves, cooking class & farewell dinner
A perfect final day — river caves, local markets, and the best meal of the trip.
Morning
Pak Ou Buddha Caves by boat
A 2-hour boat journey up the Mekong to two limestone caves packed with thousands of Buddha images. The journey itself — past fishing villages and jungle — is the main event.
$6 entry + $15 boatScenic
Afternoon
Tamarind Cooking Class
Learn to make laap, mok pa and sticky rice with Chef Joy's team at Tamarind. The 3-hour class starts at the morning market buying ingredients. One of the most memorable activities in Laos.
$35–45Highly recommended
Dinner
Farewell dinner at Tamarind Restaurant
The riverside terrace at dusk. Order the tasting menu — or lam, Mekong weeds salad, mok pa. The finest Lao cuisine in the country. Book ahead — it fills up every night.
$25–45/personBest in LP

3Day
Vientiane → Vang Vieng
Into the karst — kayaking & Blue Lagoon
3.5 hours north by bus or minivan. Arrive in Vang Vieng and spend the afternoon on the water.
Morning🚌
Bus or minivan to Vang Vieng
Take the 9am VIP bus (~$8) or hire a minivan ($12). 3.5 hours through rice paddies and limestone scenery. Arrives around lunchtime.
$8–12
Afternoon
Kayaking on the Nam Song
Rent a kayak ($5–8) and paddle the Nam Song river past the karst mountains. The scenery is extraordinary. Many operators offer guided half-day trips with cave swimming.
$5–22Highlight
Sunset
Rooftop bar with karst views
The rooftop bars along the riverside turn golden at sunset with the limestone peaks as a backdrop. Organic Farm Restaurant or Vang Vieng Boutique Resort bar.
$2–5 drink
🏨
Vang Vieng Boutique Resort
From $65/night · Nam Song river
Bus $8 from Vientiane
4Day
Vang Vieng
Caves, Blue Lagoon & organic farm lunch
The best full day in Vang Vieng — underground rivers, turquoise lagoons, and the most unique lunch in Laos.
8:00 AM
Tham Chang Cave & Blue Lagoon 1
Swim across the river to Tham Chang Cave ($1.50), then continue 6km to the Blue Lagoon — a natural swimming hole beneath a limestone cliff. Rope swing into the water.
$3–5Classic VV
Noon🌱
Lunch at Organic Mulberry Farm
Cycle 4km north through rice paddies to this working farm. Set lunch with garden-grown ingredients, mulberry wine and mulberry tea. Completely unique to Vang Vieng.
$8–12Hidden gem
Afternoon🏔
Viewpoint hike
The Pha Ngern viewpoint (1-hour hike) gives a panoramic view over the valley and river. Best light for photos is 3–4pm. Bring water — it's steep.
FreeActive
🏨
Vang Vieng Boutique Resort
2nd night Vang Vieng

Nong Khiaw
The hidden gem — limestone cliffs & river silence
3 hours by minivan from Luang Prabang. This is the Laos that most visitors never find.
Nong Khiaw limestone cliffs
Day 4 AM

3-hour drive through mountain scenery along the Nam Ou river. Arrive and check into a riverside guesthouse ($15–30).

Pha Daeng Peak viewpoint
45-minute hike to the most spectacular viewpoint in northern Laos. Two limestone karst peaks tower over the river valley. Go at golden hour.
FreeUnmissable

Long-tail boat to Muang Ngoi
40-minute boat downriver to the car-free village of Muang Ngoi. Trek to local caves, swim in the river, have lunch at a village guesthouse. Back by 4pm.

South to the Bolaven Plateau

Bolaven Coffee
Morning
Train to VTE, fly to Pakse
Train to Vientiane (2hr), then Lao Airlines flight to Pakse. Arrive by afternoon. Check in and have dinner at Delta Coffee for your first Bolaven espresso.
~$70 flight
Evening
Delta Coffee & riverside Pakse
The finest Bolaven coffee in southern Laos. Then walk the old French quarter and eat at Champa Restaurant — the best local cooking in Pakse.

9Day
Bolaven Plateau
Coffee farms, waterfalls & highland villages
Hire a motorbike or driver and spend the day on the Bolaven Plateau — one of the most rewarding day trips in Southeast Asia.
Bolaven Plateau coffee
Bolaven Plateau
7:00 AM
Bolaven Plateau loop by motorbike
Rent a semi-automatic motorbike ($12/day) or hire a driver ($60). Head to Paksong via the coffee farm road — stop at roadside stalls to buy fresh-roasted beans directly from farmers.
$12–60Must do in south
Midday
Tat Fan & Tat Yuang waterfalls
Two spectacular twin waterfalls dropping 120m through jungle. Tat Fan is the most dramatic — a viewing platform looks directly into the mist. Tat Yuang is swimmable.
$2–3 entryDramatic
Afternoon
Ethnic minority villages
The plateau is home to the Alak and Katu peoples. Several villages welcome visitors. Ask your guesthouse to arrange a respectful guided visit.
~$10 guideCultural
🏨
Pakse hotel · 2nd night
Pakse
10Day
4,000 Islands (Si Phan Don)
The Mekong's paradise — slow down completely
2 hours south of Pakse. This is Laos at its most elemental — river, hammocks, dolphins.
Don Det · Sunin Hotel ⭐
Morning
Minivan to Don Det, 4,000 Islands
2-hour bus to Nakasang, then a 5-minute boat to Don Det island. Hire a bicycle ($2/day) and ride the island loop — rice fields, stilted villages, the quietest Laos you'll find.
$8 bus + $1 boat
Dawn
Irrawaddy dolphin watching
The Mekong Irrawaddy dolphins are critically endangered — only ~80 remain. At dawn, boat to the pool near Don Khon and watch them surface in the pink morning light. One of the world's rarest wildlife sightings.
$5–8 boatOnce in a lifetime
Sunset
Sunset on the Mekong
Find a riverside bungalow hammock as the sun sets behind the Thai hills. Grilled fish, sticky rice, Beer Lao. The perfect end to 10 days in Laos.
$5–8Perfect ending
