Laos airports

Know before you fly

Laos has three international airports. Here's what you need to know about each one.

VTE
Main gateway
Wattay International Airport
Vientiane · Capital city · 3km from centre
Laos' busiest airport. Modern international terminal with direct connections to Bangkok, Singapore, Hanoi, KL, Guangzhou and beyond. Visa on arrival available. Tuk-tuk to city centre takes 10 minutes and costs around $3-5.
LPQ
UNESCO city
Luang Prabang International Airport
Luang Prabang · Heritage city · 4km from centre
Most scenic airport in SE Asia. You land surrounded by jungle and temples — extraordinary arrival. Direct flights from Bangkok, Singapore, Chiang Mai and Hanoi. Walk or tuk-tuk to most hotels in 5-15 minutes.
PKZ
Southern gateway
Pakse International Airport
Pakse · Southern Laos · 3km from centre
Gateway to the south. Best entry point for 4,000 Islands, Bolaven Plateau coffee country and Champasak ruins. Direct flights from Bangkok and Siem Reap. Small and easy to navigate — visa on arrival available.
Travel tips

Smart flying tips for Laos

💰
Book via Bangkok for best prices
The cheapest way to reach Laos from Europe, Australia or North America is to fly to Bangkok first, then take a short connecting flight to Vientiane or Luang Prabang. Bangkok Airways and Lao Airlines offer multiple daily connections.
📅
Best time to fly: October – February
Cool dry season means comfortable travel. Avoid April (Bun Pi Mai — everything closes for 3 days) unless you want to experience the water festival. March-May sees higher temperatures and cheaper flights.
🛂
Visa on arrival at all 3 airports
Most nationalities can get a visa on arrival at Wattay, Luang Prabang and Pakse airports. Cost is $30-42 USD. Bring 2 passport photos and cash. Alternatively, apply for an e-Visa at laoevisa.gov.la before you fly — saves queuing time.
✈️
Lao Airlines for domestic flights
If you're combining Vientiane and Luang Prabang, Lao Airlines and Lao Skyway operate domestic routes. The 40-minute flight beats the 10-hour bus ride significantly. Book early — domestic flights sell out during peak season.
🎒
Pack light — 20kg is usually enough
Most budget and regional airlines serving Laos allow 20kg checked baggage. Laos is hot and casual — light clothing is all you need. Laundry services are cheap and available everywhere. Avoid paying excess baggage fees.
🔄
Consider a multi-city routing
Fly into Luang Prabang and out of Vientiane (or vice versa) to avoid backtracking. Most booking platforms allow one-way in/out on the same trip. This is the smartest way to see the full country without retracing your steps.
