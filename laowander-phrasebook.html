The Basics

Start with these — a few words go a long way in Laos. Locals genuinely light up when a foreigner attempts even a single phrase.

Pronunciation key
ao = "ow" as in cow
aa = long "ah" sound
ng = can start a word (like "singing" ending)
kh = aspirated K — a soft "k" with a breath
ph = aspirated P — like "p" with a puff of air, not "f"
↗ ↘ = rising/falling tone markers

💡
Sabai dee (ສະບາຍດີ) is the single most useful word in Laos. It means hello, goodbye, how are you, and I'm doing well — all at once. Say it with a smile to every person you meet.

Quick conversion: 10,000 LAK ≈ $0.50 USD. So 100,000 LAK ≈ $5. A meal for 25,000 LAK is about $1.20 — always worth it to try the local food stalls.

Lao food is genuinely spicy. "Bor phet dae" (not spicy please) is one of the most important phrases you'll learn. Say it early and clearly — street vendors will often add chilli by default.

The traditional Lao greeting is the nop — pressing your palms together at chest height and bowing slightly. You don't need to do it, but acknowledging it when others do shows genuine respect.
